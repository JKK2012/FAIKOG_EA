Civil Engineering

-Civil engineering is a broad field that encompasses many sub-specialties. Here are some of the most common ones:

Structural Engineering: This sub-specialty focuses on the design, analysis, and construction of buildings, bridges, and other structures to ensure their safety and stability.

Transportation Engineering: This sub-specialty deals with the planning, design, construction, and maintenance of transportation systems such as roads, highways, airports, and public transit systems.

Environmental Engineering: This sub-specialty deals with the protection and improvement of the natural environment, including water and air quality, waste management, and sustainable development.

Water Resources Engineering: This sub-specialty focuses on the design and management of water resources systems, including dams, reservoirs, canals, and water treatment plants.

Coastal Engineering: This sub-specialty deals with the design and management of coastal infrastructure and resources, including beach nourishment, seawalls, and coastal flood protection.

Construction Engineering: This sub-specialty focuses on the planning, management, and supervision of construction projects, including cost estimating, scheduling, and quality control.
